USA's Most Murderous State: Louisiana

Louisianians are never “getting ready” to do something… they’re “fixin’ to do it.”

Razzle Dazzle!!! Our team of data scientist combed through thousands of rows of FBI data on murders throughout the country to find the state with the highest number of murders per 100,000 people. Not sure if we are wholly suprised by how murderous Louisiana is, but we did find the contrast when compared to the rest of the USA to be quite striking.

According to our visual, 2007 was a particularly prosperous year for the famous cemetaries of Louisiana with fresh plots popping up left and right (and all over Baton Rouge, of course -- see our Murder Map to zoom into the city). We can't say for sure what contributes to the high number of murders in Lousiana, but we suspect some of these facts might have something to do with it:
    • Drive-through daiquiris are a real thing in Louisiana
    • Britney Spears was born in Kentwood, LA
    • A specific law was passed in Louisiana to penalize people who steal crawfish. So if you’re planning a crawfish heist, maybe you should reconsider
    • In Louisiana, stealing an alligator could land a person in jail for up to ten years
    • There are almost half as many alligators as there are people in Louisiana
    • Louisiana vending machines have been known to sell Big Az Burgers, boudin, and disposable bowls of shrimp stew
    • Louisiana is home to a 3-day mud festival, the Louisiana Mudfest
    • The town of Jean Lafitte was once a hideaway town for pirates
Whatever you do, stay away from a drunken game of Razzle Dazzle or you might just end up six feet underground.
